Projects and Research

Simulation-based Analysis of Acoustic Radiation Losses in Clamped Silicon Nitride Membranes

Research Focus:

  • Quantum memory devices at room temperature (RT) preserving long-lived mechanical quantum states.
  • Ground state cooling of mechanical oscillators for quantum logic operations.
  • Reducing Radiation Pressure Noise (RPN) using backaction-evading techniques.
  • Proof-of-principle tabletop experiments for optomechanical force measurements.
  • Aiming for implementation in next-gen gravitational wave detectors.
  • Focus on Coherent Quantum Noise Cancellation (CQNC) to surpass the Standard Quantum Limit.

Silicon Nitride Membrane from Norcada Inc.:

  • Renowned for high-quality factors (Q factor) and compact sizes.
  • High mechanical Quality factor ~ 106

Radiation Pressure Noise:

  • Generated by laser interaction with Si3N4 membranes due to the Poissonian distribution of laser photons.
  • Noise arises from laser power fluctuations due to technical imperfections and quantum noise, leading to small, random displacements in the membrane.

Energy Loss Mechanisms:

  • Thermal noise
  • Gas damping
  • Material losses
  • Acoustic radiation loss (phonon-tunneling loss)

Phonon-Tunneling Loss: A method introduced for computing acoustic radiation loss using the Perfectly Matched Layer (PML) concept to absorb incoming waves.

Simulation and Design

The project introduced a method for computing acoustic radiation loss using COMSOL Multiphysics for finite element analysis. Key components include:

  • Iterative model improvements and issue resolution in COMSOL.
  • Utilization of PML for effective absorption of waves.
  • Simulation results aligned with experimental references, validating the methodology.

The research demonstrated successful reduction of acoustic radiation losses, with potential applications in quantum devices and next-generation gravitational wave detectors.
